Canes respond with gritty one-goal win: Canes 3, Pink Whitney 2

A tighter, more disciplined performance showed the Canes can win close — even when the finish isn’t pretty.

By Rinkside Staff
• Jan 19, 2026
• 3 min read
Key takeaways
  • The Canes tightened their structure after a Week 1 track meet and were rewarded.
  • Michael Brown again kept the floor high while the skaters found enough offense.
  • Winning close is a skill — and the Canes took a step toward owning it.

A week after a high-event opener got away from them, the Canes flipped the script. They edged Pink Whitney 3–2 in Week 2, leaning on pace, effort, and just enough timely finishing to bank two points.

This one looked more like a team that learned its lesson: fewer reckless exchanges, better tracking through the neutral zone, and a willingness to grind out shifts when space disappeared.

Michael Brown was steady again, and the Canes did the little things that win one-goal games — getting bodies back, winning board battles, and surviving the uncomfortable minutes where momentum feels one bounce away from flipping.

It wasn’t perfect — it rarely is in this league — but it was the kind of response that keeps seasons from drifting early.

Bottom line: the Canes didn’t just win. They showed they can win the kind of game they’ll see most often.
