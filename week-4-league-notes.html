Week 4 League Notes: Structure wins, chaos leaks points

Three games, three very different scripts — and one consistent lesson: teams that stayed patient got paid.

By Rinkside Staff • League Notebook • Week 4

Week 4 didn’t reshape the standings dramatically, but it clarified something important: the league is splitting less by talent and more by tolerance for chaos. The teams that managed games walked away with points. The teams that didn’t were left wondering how a winnable night slipped away.

Week 4 results

Chanclas 2, Banditos 0
Owls 1, Canes 0
Pink Whitney 7, Fridge Raiders 5

Chanclas vs Banditos: The standard holds

The Chanclas did what they’ve done all season: took a game that could’ve gotten loose and refused to let it. A 2–0 win over the Banditos wasn’t flashy, but it was authoritative. No extended scrambling. No momentum swings. Just structure, discipline, and patience.

“They wait you out,” said Banditos winger Ricky “Ruckus” Ramirez. “You try to do too much and suddenly you’re chasing.”

For the Banditos, it was another reminder that effort alone doesn’t solve problems. Until they can slow games down, they’ll continue to live on the wrong side of these nights.

Owls vs Canes: One bounce, one result

The Owls’ 1–0 win over the Canes fit both teams’ early-season profiles perfectly. Low event. Few clean looks. One early bounce — and then a game closed out by goaltending.

The Canes controlled large stretches of play and generated pressure, but the Owls were comfortable living in a narrow game. For the Owls, it was proof that staying connected defensively can still pay dividends.

“We weren’t trying to win it 5–4,” said Owls captain Graham O’Neill. “We just needed one.”

Pink Whitney vs Fridge Raiders: Chaos reigns

If you were looking for order, this wasn’t the game. Pink Whitney’s 7–5 win over the Fridge Raiders was the highest-event matchup of the week — a reminder that when neither team wants to slow down, the scoreboard usually reflects it.

Pink Whitney’s offensive talent remains undeniable, but the same questions persist for both sides: what happens when the game tightens?

“That’s on us,” said Fridge Raiders forward Kevin “Kegs” Malloy. “We know better than to trade chances all night.”

Week 4 power rankings (editorial)

1. Chanclas
The league’s north star. Structured, calm, and comfortable winning ugly.
2. Canes
The process is strong. The margin for error is thin. Finishing remains the separator.
3. Pink Whitney
Offense travels. Structure still doesn’t — but seven goals fix a lot of moods.
4. Fridge Raiders
High ceiling, low floor. Until pace control improves, volatility stays.
5. Owls
Proof of concept defensively. Now comes the harder part: repeating it.
6. Banditos
Effort is there. Discipline and patience still lag.

Looking ahead

Week 4 reinforced a simple truth: no one is running away with this league. But patience is starting to separate teams who can survive the grind from teams who keep trying to shortcut it.

Week 5 won’t crown a contender — but it will punish anyone still hoping chaos breaks their way.
