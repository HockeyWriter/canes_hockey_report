Statement night: Canes blank Banditos 5–0

After living on thin margins early, the Canes put together their most complete game — a five-goal outburst paired with a shutout.

By Rinkside Staff
• Jan 26, 2026
• 3 min read
Key takeaways
  • The Canes dominated all three zones and never let the game get chaotic.
  • Michael Brown anchored the result, turning a strong effort into a shutout.
  • This was the blueprint: forecheck, structure, and offense coming in waves.

The Canes didn’t just win in Week 3 — they controlled. A 5–0 shutout of the Banditos served as the team’s first true “complete game” of the season, pairing offensive pressure with defensive discipline.

The difference from Week 1’s track meet was obvious: the Canes stayed connected through the neutral zone, kept pucks alive below the dots, and forced the Banditos into long shifts where mistakes became inevitable.

Once the Canes got rolling, the scoring came in waves. And on the nights when the skaters are doing their job, Michael Brown is more than enough to close the door.

Three weeks in, the Canes’ season is already defined by variance — but Week 3 was the reminder of the ceiling. If they can bottle that structure, this team is going to be a problem for everyone in the standings.
