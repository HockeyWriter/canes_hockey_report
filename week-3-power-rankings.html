Week 3 Power Rankings: early tiers emerge in the Advanced League

Three weeks in, the standings are starting to form into tiers — one clear front-runner, a crowded middle with real volatility, and a bottom group that’s closer than the records suggest.

By Rinkside Staff
• Winter 2026
• 5 min read
Key takeaways
  • Chanclas are the early standard: undefeated, structured, low drama.
  • The middle tier (Canes, Fridge Raiders, Pink Whitney) can beat anyone — and lose to anyone.
  • Owls and Banditos are closer than the results show, but details are costing them points.

Power rankings aren’t standings — they’re a temperature check. Through Week 3, one thing is clear: the league isn’t separating into “good” and “bad.” It’s separating into consistent and volatile.

“Three weeks in, the league’s identity is parity — but the teams that avoid chaos are already getting paid in points.”
— Week 3 rinkside notes

Power Rankings (Through Week 3 results)

1. Chanclas
Record: 3–0 (projected)

The early pace-setters. Wins over Owls (4–2), Banditos (2–0), and Pink Whitney (4–1) show a team that can win different kinds of games without changing its personality. They defend, they manage the puck, and they don’t donate odd-man rushes.

Trend: Quietly dominant. No wasted shifts.
2. Canes
Record: 2–1 (projected)

The ceiling is obvious: a 5–0 shutout over the Banditos is the most complete performance we’ve seen from any team. But the floor is real too (see: a 5–4 loss to the Fridge Raiders). When the Canes stay structured, they’re a top-tier problem. When games get loose, they live in coin-flip territory.

Trend: High ceiling, thin margins. If finishing normalizes, they can chase #1.
3. Fridge Raiders
Record: 2–1 (projected)

The league’s volatility kings. They outgun the Canes 5–4, then get outscored by Pink Whitney 6–3, then win a 1–0 grinder over the Owls. Their range of outcomes is the widest in the league — which makes them dangerous and exhausting in equal measure.

Trend: They can play any style… but don’t always choose one.
4. Pink Whitney
Record: 1–2 (projected)

If you’re looking for offense, this is the team. The 6–3 win over the Fridge Raiders shows the firepower is real. The problem is the rest of the story: losses to Chanclas (4–1) and Canes (3–2) suggest they’re still searching for the balance that wins tight games.

Trend: Scary with space. Still learning how to close.
5. Banditos
Record: 1–2 (projected)

The Banditos have already shown both extremes: shut out twice (0–5 vs Canes, 0–2 vs Chanclas) and then a 5–4 win over the Owls that proved they can score enough to win if the game opens up. The issue is consistency — defensively, shift-to-shift.

Trend: Capable of offense, but can’t afford the lapses.
6. Owls
Record: 0–3 (projected)

The record is ugly. The games aren’t. A 5–4 loss to the Banditos and a 1–0 loss to the Fridge Raiders are the definition of “one bounce away,” and even the 4–2 loss to Chanclas was competitive. But moral victories don’t move you up the table.

Trend: Better than the record — but running out of runway.

Game of the week

Fridge Raiders 5, Canes 4 — the early-season measuring stick for “how chaotic can this league get?” The Canes proved they can trade offense with anyone. The Raiders proved they can survive a track meet.

What to watch next

The league’s middle tier is where the season will be decided. If Chanclas are the standard, the real question is which team can stay structured long enough to challenge them — and which teams will keep donating points in one-goal games.
