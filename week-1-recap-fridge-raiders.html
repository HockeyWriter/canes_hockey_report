Canes drop opener in shootout-style track meet: Fridge Raiders 5, Canes 4

A fast-paced Week 1 game exposed the Canes’ early identity: they can score with anyone — but the margins are thin when the pace gets wild.

By Rinkside Staff
• Jan 12, 2026
• 3 min read
Key takeaways
  • The Canes proved they can trade punches offensively, but defensive structure lagged in the opener.
  • Michael Brown kept it within reach, but the game lived in high-danger moments.
  • Early theme: finishing chances matters less than avoiding the one extra mistake.

The Canes opened the season with a four-goal night and still skated off on the wrong side of the scoreboard, falling 5–4 to the Fridge Raiders in a game that felt more like a roller coaster than a systems clinic.

From the start, the pace was loose and opportunistic — quick transition chances, broken plays turning into rushes, and long stretches where the puck lived in the middle of the ice. The Canes matched the Raiders shift for shift offensively, but the difference was a familiar one in games like this: one extra breakdown.

Michael Brown battled through traffic and second chances to keep it close, and the Canes never stopped pushing. But in a game defined by momentum swings, the Canes couldn’t quite land the final counterpunch.

The encouraging part for the Canes: the offense showed up immediately. The warning: when games turn into track meets, the line between “fun win” and “frustrating loss” is usually just one missed assignment.

Week 1 was proof the Canes have enough to compete with anyone. Week 2 would be the first test of whether they can tighten the screws.

Final score

Fridge Raiders 5
Canes 4

Theme

High-event hockey. One mistake too many.
